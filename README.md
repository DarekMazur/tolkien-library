# Tolkien Library – Polish Editions & Related Works

## Project Overview

**Tolkien Library** is a web project dedicated to cataloging and presenting comprehensive information about Polish editions of works by J.R.R. Tolkien, as well as publications about the author (such as biographies and critical studies) and related Tolkien-inspired literature. The goal is to create a centralized, accessible resource for readers, researchers, and collectors interested in the Polish Tolkien publishing landscape.

## Key Features

- **Database of Polish Editions**: Detailed records of all known Polish editions of Tolkien’s works, including translators, publishers, publication dates, and cover images.
- **Publications About Tolkien**: Listings and descriptions of biographies, essays, and studies about J.R.R. Tolkien published in Poland.
- **Tolkien-Related Publications**: Information on books inspired by Tolkien’s legendarium or scholarship, including fan publications and academic works.
- **Search & Filter**: Tools to help users find specific editions, authors, or topics of interest.
- **Resource for Collectors and Fans**: A reference point for those seeking to expand their collections or deepen their knowledge of Tolkien’s presence in Polish culture.

## Motivation

The project aims to fill a gap in accessible, well-organized information on Tolkien’s works and related literature as published in Poland. While Tolkien’s books have been translated and published in many countries, Polish editions are notable for their diversity, unique translations, and rich publishing history. This site seeks to document and celebrate that legacy.

- **Linting & Formatting**: ESLint, Prettier, Husky, lint-staged
